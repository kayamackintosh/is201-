Ted Lasso's Iconic Pop Culture References

Welcome to Ted Lasso's pop culture run-down, the go to page for all your favorite pop culture references in Seasons 1 and 2 of the hit Apple TV series. Whether you’re a movie lover, a musical enthusiast, or just curious about the hottest pop culture quotes, our website brings together all the iconic pop culture references into one spot so you don't miss a thing. Stay ahead of the curve with our insider guide to every single pop culture reference so that you don't miss any of the easter eggs placed by the writers. From arts & entertainment to movie quotes and sports references, Ted Lasso brings humor and personality to his interactions with other characters throughout both seasons. Our interface allows you to browse through each of Ted's references, organized by season, country of origin, categories, and more. Whether you're testing your pop culture knowledge or challenging yourself to see how many you can catch, we provide detailed insights into the references of each episode, including the direct quote, the episode it appears in, the original source, and a bried explanation of the context.

  1. "You've Got Mail"
    • Reference Type: Movie
    • Released: 1998
    • Notable: Ted compares Rebecca and Sam's anonymous texting to the film: "It’s like that movie—you’ve got mail! Except it’s more like... you’ve got flirty banter."
  2. "The Empire Strikes Back"
    • Reference Type: Movie
    • Released: 1980
    • Notable: When facing a tough moment, Ted reflects: "It’s like when Luke found out Vader was his father. That was a tough day for all of us."
  3. "Allen Iverson 'We're talking about practice' speech"
    • Reference Type: Sports Moment
    • Year: 2002
    • Notable: Ted mimics Iverson’s rant: "We talkin’ 'bout practice. Not the game. Practice."
  4. "The Sound of Music"
    • Reference Type: Movie/Musical
    • Released: 1965
    • Notable: Ted sings while exiting a room: “So long, farewell, auf Wiedersehen, goodnight!”
  5. "Lady Gaga"
    • Reference Type: Music/Pop Culture
    • Era: 2000s–Present
    • Notable: Ted encourages confidence and individuality: "Be like Lady Gaga, y’know? Just dance it out, baby."
  6. "Rom-Communism"
    • Reference Type: Philosophy + Romantic Comedies
    • Origin: Ted Lasso (Season 2)
    • Notable: Ted's optimistic life outlook: “I believe in Communism. Rom-Communism, that is. If all those attractive people with their amazing apartments and witty banter can go through some light-hearted struggles and still end up happy? Then so can we.”
